Brest is a port city in the Finistère department, Brittany.

With 139,456 inhabitants (2020), Brest forms Western Brittany's largest metropolitan area (with a population of 370,000 in total), ranking third behind only Nantes and Rennes in the whole of historic Brittany, and the 25th most populous city in France (2019).

During the Middle Ages, the history of Brest was the history of its castle. Then Richelieu made it a military harbour in 1631.

Brest's history has since the 17th century been linked to the sea: the Académie de Marine (Naval Academy) was founded in 1752 in this city.
